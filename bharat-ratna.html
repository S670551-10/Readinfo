First Indian to win Bharat Ratna

The Bharat Ratna is the highest civilian award of India. It was started in 1954 to honor people who gave great service to the nation in different fields. In the very first year, three famous Indians received this award: C. Rajagopalachari, Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan, and Sir C. V. Raman.

Winners (1954)

C. Rajagopalachari

Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan

C. V. Raman

C. Rajagopalachari : C. Rajagopalachari, also known as Rajaji, was a great freedom fighter and leader. He was the last Governor-General of India after independence. He worked for the country with honesty and simplicity.

Sir C. V. Raman : A famous scientist in Physics. He discovered the Raman Effect in light and won the Nobel Prize in 1930. His work made India proud in the field of science.

Summary

The Bharat Ratna award began in 1954. The first awardees were C. Rajagopalachari, Dr. S. Radhakrishnan, and Sir C. V. Raman. They were honored for their contributions in politics, education, and science.
